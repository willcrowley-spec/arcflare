## Design Context

### Users
Enterprise IT leaders, VPs of IT, CTOs, Salesforce architects, and platform strategy consultants. They use Arcflare during org assessments, merger evaluations, and automation roadmap planning — typically on a laptop in a corporate setting. They are data-literate, skeptical of fluff, and expect tools that look like they belong next to Datadog or Snowflake, not a Dribbble shot.

### Brand Personality
**Authoritative. Precise. Quietly confident.**

Arcflare is an enterprise intelligence platform, not a consumer SaaS product. It should feel like a tool built by people who understand the problem deeply — not one that's trying to impress with visual tricks. The UI should earn trust through clarity, density of information, and surgical precision in layout.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Corporate-professional with a sharp edge. Clean without being sterile. Dense without being cluttered.
- **Theme**: Light mode for the authenticated app (gray-50 body, white surfaces). Dark navy header as the brand anchor. Orange as the sole accent color.
- **Color palette**: Custom navy scale (navy-50 through navy-900) for brand identity. Slate for neutral text hierarchy. Orange-400/500 for CTAs and active states. Emerald/amber/red for semantic status indicators only.
- **References**: Datadog dashboards (information density done right), Linear (clean enterprise UI), Snowflake console (authoritative data platform feel).
- **Anti-references**: Generic Tailwind UI templates, Dribbble "dashboard concept" shots, anything with glassmorphism or purple gradients.

### Design Principles
1. **Information density over whitespace theater** — Enterprise users want data, not decorative padding. Every pixel should earn its place.
2. **Hierarchy through typography and spacing, not decoration** — Use font weight, size contrast, and spatial rhythm to create clarity. Avoid decorative borders, gratuitous icons, or visual noise.
3. **Trust through consistency** — Same patterns everywhere. Cards look the same. Tables behave the same. Status indicators are predictable. No surprises.
4. **Functional color only** — Orange is the accent. Emerald/amber/red are status. That's it. No color for decoration.
5. **Respect the user's intelligence** — No redundant labels, no "welcome to your dashboard" copy, no tooltips that explain obvious things.
